## Organization Design

### Organization Structure Design Principles

1. **Strategy-Driven** - Organization structure serves strategic objectives
2. **Simple & Efficient** - Flat hierarchy, avoid over-complexity
3. **Clear Authority & Responsibility** - Each role has well-defined authority and accountability
4. **Flexible & Adaptable** - Able to respond quickly to market changes

### Common Organization Structure Types

| Type | Characteristics | Applicable Scenarios | Pros & Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Functional** | Departments organized by function | Stable environments, standardized processes | ✅High specialization ❌Weak cross-functional collaboration |
| **Divisional** | Organized by product/region | Diversified businesses, large-scale enterprises | ✅High flexibility ❌Resource duplication |
| **Matrix** | Dual reporting: function + project | Project-driven, resource sharing required | ✅High resource utilization ❌Complex dual reporting |
| **Flat** | Reduced hierarchy, delegation of authority | Innovative, fast-changing industries | ✅Fast decision-making ❌Wide span of control |
| **Network** | Outsourcing + core team | Asset-light, flexible workforce | ✅Flexible ❌Weak control |

### Organization Design Process

1. **Diagnose Current State** - Analyze issues with current structure
2. **Clarify Strategy** - Understand organizational requirements from strategy
3. **Design Solution** - Design new organization structure
4. **Pilot Validation** - Small-scale pilot testing
5. **Full Rollout** - Phased implementation
6. **Continuous Optimization** - Adjust based on effectiveness

## Culture & Engagement

### Engagement Drivers

According to Gallup research, key factors influencing engagement:

| Rank | Driver | Impact | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Relationship with Manager | 40% | Direct supervisor is the biggest influencer of engagement |
| 2 | Career Development Opportunities | 25% | Growth space and learning opportunities |
| 3 | Meaningful Work | 20% | Understanding work value |
| 4 | Recognition & Praise | 15% | Timely recognition and feedback |
| 5 | Work-Life Balance | 12% | Reasonable workload |
| 6 | Compensation & Benefits | 10% | Fair compensation (hygiene factor) |

### Engagement Improvement Interventions

**High-Impact Measures:**
1. **Manager Training** - Enhance management capabilities (coaching, feedback skills)
2. **One-on-Ones** - Regular in-depth communication (monthly/quarterly)
3. **Career Development Planning** - Clear promotion paths and development plans
4. **Work Autonomy** - Give employees more decision-making authority

**Medium-Impact Measures:**
1. **Team Building Activities** - Enhance team cohesion
2. **Recognition Mechanisms** - Timely praise and rewards
3. **Work Optimization** - Reduce meaningless work
4. **Flexible Work** - Remote work, flexible hours

**Foundational Measures:**
1. **Fair Compensation** - Market-aligned compensation levels
2. **Good Environment** - Comfortable work environment
3. **Benefits & Security** - Comprehensive benefits system

### Culture Change Methodology

**Kotter's 8-Step Change Model:**
1. Establish a sense of urgency
2. Form a powerful coalition
3. Create a vision for change
4. Communicate the vision
5. Empower employees
6. Generate short-term wins
7. Consolidate gains
8. Anchor the change

**Success Factors for Culture Change:**
- Executive commitment (essential)
- Full employee participation
- Continuous communication
- Behavior institutionalization (systems, processes)
- Patience and persistence (culture change requires 2-3 years)

## Talent & Performance

### Performance Management Best Practices

**From Traditional Appraisal to Continuous Performance Management:**

| Traditional Performance Management | Modern Performance Management |
|---|---|
| Annual appraisal | Continuous feedback |
| Rating and ranking | Development-oriented |
| Top-down | Two-way communication |
| Past-focused | Future-oriented |
| Forced distribution | Individualized |

**OKR vs KPI:**

| Dimension | OKR | KPI |
|---|---|---|
| Position | Goal management tool | Performance appraisal tool |
| Characteristics | Challenging, measurable | Minimum requirements |
| Quantity | 3-5 objectives | 5-8 indicators |
| Cycle | Quarterly | Annual/Monthly |
| Transparency | Fully transparent | Partially transparent |
| Link to Compensation | Not directly linked | Directly linked |

**Best Practices:**
- Combine OKR and KPI: Use OKR for goal management, KPI for performance appraisal
- Regular check-ins (weekly/biweekly): Track progress, adjust timely
- 360-degree feedback: Multi-angle assessment, but pay attention to design quality

### Compensation Design Principles

1. **External Equity** - Benchmark against market, maintain competitiveness
2. **Internal Equity** - Equal pay for equal work, value alignment
3. **Individual Equity** - Performance differentiation, reflect contribution
4. **Transparency** - Transparent compensation structure and rules
5. **Flexibility** - Able to adjust based on market conditions

**Compensation Structure:**
- Fixed Compensation: Base salary (security)
- Variable Compensation: Performance bonus/annual bonus (incentive)
- Benefits & Allowances: Insurance, allowances, benefits (care)
- Long-term Incentives: Options, equity (retention)

## Change Management

### Change Management Framework

**ADKAR Model (Individual Level):**
- **A**wareness - Recognizing the need for change
- **D**esire - Willingness to participate in change
- **K**nowledge - Knowing how to change
- **A**bility - Being able to implement change
- **R**einforcement - Reinforcing new behaviors

**Three-Phase Change Management (Organizational Level):**
1. **Ending Phase** - Letting go of old ways
   - Acknowledge losses
   - Understand resistance
   - Maintain communication

2. **Neutral Phase** - Transition period
   - Provide support
   - Quick decision-making
   - Temporary structures

3. **New Beginning Phase** - New normal
   - Celebrate success
   - Institutionalize new behaviors
   - Continuous follow-up

### Resistance Management

**Root Causes of Resistance:**
- Fear of the unknown
- Lack of capability
- Loss of benefits
- Lack of trust
- Insufficient communication

**Response Strategies:**
1. **Adequate Communication** - Explain why, what, and how
2. **Participatory Design** - Involve employees in change design
3. **Provide Support** - Training, coaching, resources
4. **Quick Wins** - Create short-term successes
5. **Leadership Modeling** - Lead by example
