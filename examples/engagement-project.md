# Employee Engagement Improvement Project - SuperHROD Usage Example

This document demonstrates how to use SuperHROD to plan a complete employee engagement improvement project.

## Project Background

The Technology Department of a tech company (200 employees) has seen employee engagement decline for two consecutive years, dropping from 72 to 65 points. HR needs to plan an engagement improvement initiative.

---

## Step 1: Requirements Clarification

**User Input:**
```
Our Technology Department's engagement has declined for two consecutive years, and we want to launch an improvement project.
```

**SuperHROD Conversational Inquiry:**

> SuperHROD: Understood. Let me ask a few questions to clarify the project requirements.
>
> **Question 1/7:** How many people are in the Technology Department? Who are the key stakeholders?
>
> User: Technology Department has 200 people. The overseeing leader is VP of Technology Mr. Zhang, and HRD is Ms. Li.
>
> **Question 2/7:** How much has engagement declined? Do you have specific data or survey reports?
>
> User: Last year it dropped from 72 to 65 points. Our annual survey data shows the main issues are "management communication" and "workload."
>
> **Question 3/7:** What is the expected timeline for project completion? 3 months, 6 months, or 1 year?
>
> User: 6 months. We hope to see results by the end of this year.
>
> **Question 4/7:** What is the approximate budget range?
>
> User: Around 200,000 RMB, mainly for training and activities.
>
> **Question 5/7:** What are the primary objectives of this project? What outcomes are expected?
>
> User: We hope to restore engagement to 70 points by year-end and improve the management climate in the Technology Department.
>
> **Question 6/7:** What are the biggest concerns or risks?
>
> User: The technical team is very busy and may resist additional time investment. Also, some technical leaders lack management experience.
>
> **Question 7/7:** How many HR team members are involved in this project? Is there external support?
>
> User: I (HR BP) am primarily responsible, with one HR assistant supporting. No external consultants for now.

**SuperHROD Generates Requirements Summary:**

```markdown
## Project Requirements Summary

**Project Type:** Employee Engagement Improvement
**Project Name:** Technology Department Engagement Improvement Project

### Basic Information
- **Scope:** Technology Department (200 employees)
- **Objective:** Improve engagement from 65 to 70 points within 6 months, enhance management climate
- **Timeline:** July 2024 - December 2024
- **Budget:** Approximately 200,000 RMB

### Stakeholders
- **Sponsor:** Ms. Li (HRD)
- **Decision Maker:** Mr. Zhang (VP of Technology)
- **Core Team:** HR BP (Lead), HR Assistant

### Constraints
- **Time Constraint:** Results needed by year-end
- **Budget Constraint:** 200,000 RMB
- **Resource Constraint:** HR team of 2, no external consultants

### Key Risks & Concerns
- Technical team is busy and may resist additional investment
- Some technical leaders have insufficient management experience

### Success Criteria
- Year-end engagement survey reaches 70 points
- Management communication issues improved
- Technical team participation rate > 80%
```

## Step 2: Solution Design

**SuperHROD Conducts Internal and External Information Gathering:**

Internal queries:
- Searching for historical similar projects...
- Querying best practices repository...

External search (MCP):
- "Employee engagement improvement tech company 2024"
- "Gallup engagement management communication"
- "Google Project Oxygen management behaviors"

**SuperHROD Presents Solution in Segments:**

### Segment 1: Problem Diagnosis and Root Cause Analysis

> Based on your description and survey data, the root causes of engagement decline in the Technology Department may include:
>
> 1. **Insufficient Management Communication** - Technical leaders lack management skills, communication frequency and quality are inadequate
> 2. **Excessive Workload** - Technical team is under high pressure and lacks support
> 3. **Unclear Career Development Paths** - Engineers cannot see growth trajectories
>
> **External Research Support:**
> - Gallup 2024 Study: Relationship with managers is the #1 driver of engagement (40% impact)
> - Google Project Oxygen: Regular feedback and communication is one of the top 8 behaviors of effective managers
>
> Recommendation for in-depth diagnosis: Focus group interviews (10-15 people) + supplementary questionnaire
>
> **Does this part look acceptable? Any adjustments needed?**

User: Looks good, continue.

### Segment 2: Intervention Design

> Based on root cause analysis, design 3 core interventions:
>
> **Intervention 1: Technical Leader Management Capability Enhancement**
> - Content: Coaching skills, feedback techniques, one-on-one meetings
> - Format: 2-day workshop + monthly practice salon
> - Target: 20 technical leads
>
> **Industry Case References:**
> - Google: Internal manager training program emphasizing "be a coach, not a boss"
> - Alibaba: "Management Three Axes" training covering all managers
>
> **Localization Adjustments:**
> - Considering the technical team's busy schedule, adopt lightweight 2-hour monthly salons
> - Provide ready-to-use one-on-one meeting templates to lower usage barriers
>
> **Intervention 2: Regular One-on-One Meeting Mechanism**
> - Content: Managers conduct 30-minute one-on-one communication with subordinates monthly
> - Assurance: Provide meeting templates and training, HR tracks execution rate
>
> **Intervention 3: Work Optimization and Support**
> - Content: Prioritize work, reduce low-value tasks; increase personnel support
>
> **Is this part acceptable?**

User: Good, what about the third segment?

### Segment 3: Implementation Plan Overview

> The project is divided into 4 phases:
>
> **Phase 1: Preparation (July, 2 weeks)**
> - Assemble project team
> - In-depth diagnosis (focus group interviews)
>
> **Phase 2: Intervention Design (July-August, 4 weeks)**
> - Design workshop content
> - Prepare meeting templates
>
> **Phase 3: Implementation (August-November, 16 weeks)**
> - Technical Leader training
> - Roll out one-on-one meetings
> - Monthly practice salons
>
> **Phase 4: Evaluation (December, 4 weeks)**
> - Impact evaluation
> - Project retrospective
>
